RoadMent

An end-to-end Road Segmentation Platform


Road segmentation in aerial images is a crucial process in the workflow of target based aerial image analysis. This task focusses to mapping every pixel to a semantic label. It has various applications such as road navigation, urban infrastructure development and geographic information collection.In the last decade, a lot of research has happened related to road information extraction from aerial imagery.
To achieve this task, we will use a deep learning model inspired by the U-Net architecture. U-Net is a encoder-decoder based model, constructed by using fully convolutional and pooling layers, which combines low level and high level semantic features. Moreover existing technologies such as Google Maps rely on human effort for annotation of their maps. Our project aims at automating the task of pointing out the roads in such maps, which require frequent updates and checking.
The model will be trained on MIT Road Dataset and the roads will be masked in green color on top of the original image, thus indicating the complete road network in the image. The dataset is primarily based on the satellite images of Boston, USA, but the model will try to learn the necessary features and will be able to generalize its predictions to images of other cities too. This project will be the first attempt to build an end-to-end, user friendly platform for segmentation of roads.
Image segmentation is the division of an image into regions or categories, which correspond to different objects or parts of objects. Every pixel in an image is allocated to one of a number of these categories. It is used in a wide variety of fields ranging from medical imaging to self-driving cars. We aim to extend this idea to segment out the roads from satellite images.
